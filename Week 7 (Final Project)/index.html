Welcome to Operation Origami!

Have you ever finished an origami project and thought to yourself, “what’s next?” Maybe you wanted to fold something, but couldn’t manage to find a tutorial? Well you’re in the right place, because here at Operation Origami we have it all! We have a large documentation of origami creations, complete with pictures, descriptions, and links to tutorials.

Normal Origami

These are your average, run-of-the-mill origami projects. These will tend to be more flat and compact, as well as a bit easier than some of the more advanced 3D models. If you're a beginner, or looking for an easier project, this category is the place for you.

3D Origami

As the name suggests, these projects take up a bit more space. Whether through many pieces or one paper, these models make for nice decorations. Take caution with these projects though, because not only are they harder to make, but they are also much more time consuming. Lots of time and effort went into most of these, so try them out yourselves if you're up for a challenge.

Origami Jewelry

These projects don’t really have tutorials, since I took a simple design and made creative liberties. There are tutorials provided for the basic model, but if you wish to recreate these, you’ll have to trust in your artistic abilities. Alternatively, if you would like to browse some options that are up for purchase, visit my Etsy shop to check it out!

Miscellaneous Projects

These projects are models that don’t quite fit in anywhere. Whether they’re not completely flat or 3D, or if they’re other art mediums with origami incorporated, these ones don’t have tutorials. This category is mostly just for misfit projects. Look at the pictures, read the description, and take inspiration from the ones you like.
